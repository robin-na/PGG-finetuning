Across 20 shared chat-enabled validation games, both simulated chat variants over-communicate relative to humans, but in different ways. Humans average 3.34 messages per game-round and only 39% of players speak in a given round, versus 6.85 / 85% for oracle and 7.24 / 88% for retrieved. Humans are also much less templated: 91% of human messages are unique, compared with 73% for both model variants.

- Humans use chat reactively: 39% of messages happen in outcome screens, 33% in summary screens, and only 27% in contribution screens.
- Both model variants use chat like a pre-action control channel, with very heavy coordination language: 89% of oracle messages and 91% of retrieved messages are coordination-oriented, versus 20% for humans.
- Retrieved is the most scripted around “full contribution”: 63% of its messages explicitly mention 20/full/max, versus 48% for oracle and 8% for humans.
- Human chat contains much more banter and identity/meta talk, while oracle sounds more like compliance enforcement and retrieved sounds more like a polished but repetitive coordination script.

Representative messages:
- Human: "We earn more if we all work together!", "lol"
- Oracle: "Quick contributions please.", "All contribute 20. Or I will punish."
- Retrieved: "Hello everyone. Contributing fully this round.", "20. Let's see how this goes."
