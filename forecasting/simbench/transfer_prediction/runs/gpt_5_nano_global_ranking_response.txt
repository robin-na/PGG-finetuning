Explanation
The Twin-2K-500 source provides rich per-person priors (demographics, trait scores, cognitive summaries, economic preferences, and open-ended content) for a single twin, not access to held-out target answers. This makes the external persona most useful for tasks where individual-difference signals (personality, cognition, preferences) can meaningfully shape predictions about an individual’s responses or where there is a direct link between those priors and task outcomes. Consequently:
- Strongly aligned tasks (where predictions depend on personality/traits or individual preferences) are most likely to benefit: Big Five (OSPsychBig5), RWAS (Open-Source Right-Wing Authoritarianism; political/ideology-oriented items), MACH-IV (Machiavellianism), MGKT (general knowledge with cultural context), and also risk-preference/analytic items like Choices13k and NumberGame.
- Tasks involving interpretable distributions across many groups or country-specific context (GlobalOpinionQA, ISSP) or broad safety/commonsense distributions (DICES, ChaosNLI) are less likely to gain from a single individual's priors; some may even mislead if the persona mismatches the target population. To reflect the uncertainty, several of these are predicted as insignificant, with a couple of broader cross-national or ideology-focused tasks labeled negative due to potential mismatch with a single US twin profile.
- Some tasks that are more problem-solving or generic ( WisdomOfCrowds, Jester, etc.) may see only modest, mixed effects.

Ranking and labels are designed to reflect such relative alignment, not exact scores, given we cannot rely on actual benchmark results here.
