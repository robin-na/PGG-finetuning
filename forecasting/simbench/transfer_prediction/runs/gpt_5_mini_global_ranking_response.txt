Summary of reasoning principles used
- Twin-2K-500 supplies rich, U.S.-based person-level priors that are especially informative for traits that map directly onto task behavior: economic preferences and behavioral-game tendencies, cognitive ability for reasoning tasks, demographics and personality for many opinion and value judgments, and some open-ended/self-description signals that can proxy ideology or taste.
- Transfer will be most useful when (a) the target task responses are plausibly driven by stable individual traits that Twin measures (risk attitudes, cognition, personality, basic demographics), (b) the target population is U.S.-based (good match), and (c) task items are preference/attitude- or trait-driven rather than domain-specific factual knowledge or highly context-specific institutional wording.
- Transfer will be less helpful or negligible when responses depend on domain-specific knowledge, platform-specific sample-frame idiosyncrasies, survey-wave / institutional context, or extreme item-specific ambiguity that overwhelms person-level trait signals.

Task-level, concrete reasoning

- Choices13k — positive (high confidence)
  - Strong match: Twin includes 10 economic-preference tests, within- and between-subject behavioral-economics experiments, and pricing/purchase decisions. Risk/time preferences and measured behavioral tendencies should directly predict lottery choices and shift the population choice distribution. Good U.S. population match and item structure (explicit incentives) increase transfer usefulness.

- NumberGame — positive (high confidence)
  - Strongly depends on inductive and similarity-based reasoning; Twin provides detailed cognitive-ability summaries (11 cognitive tests). Cognitive ability correlates closely with generalization and rule-inference performance, so persona priors should materially improve predicting per-person judgments and aggregate distributions.

- OpinionQA — positive (high confidence)
  - OpinionQA targets U.S. public-opinion distributions across demographic groups. Twin supplies demographics, trait scores, and preference summaries. Those variables are exactly what drives many opinion questions, so persona priors should help match human opinion distributions.

- WisdomOfCrowds — positive (moderately high confidence)
  - Many items are problem-solving with objectively correct answers; Twin cognitive summaries and demographics should help predict who answers correctly and thus improve population distributions on these tasks.

- ConspiracyCorr — positive (moderate confidence)
  - Conspiracy endorsement is strongly associated with demographics, personality, and cognitive factors. Twin's demographic and personality measures (and likely items relating to political attitudes in open-ended content) should help predict belief distributions in U.S. respondents.

- MoralMachine — positive (moderate confidence)
  - Moral tradeoffs track stable value preferences and demographic/cultural patterns. Twin measures personality and preference summaries that can map to utilitarian vs. deontological tendencies and demographic correlates of moral choices, so helpful transfer is expected.

- GlobalOpinionQA — positive (moderate confidence)
  - Although cross-national in origin, the SimBench slice is U.S.-focused. Twin demographics and values are relevant to many public-opinion items, so persona priors should help, albeit less than for OpinionQA because question sets are varied and smaller in row count.

- TISP — positive (modest confidence)
  - Trust in science and climate attitudes are value-laden and tied to demographics and political outlook. Twin can provide partial predictive signal, but these items are strongly entangled with survey-wave/institutional context and complex framing, reducing transfer magnitude.

- OSPsychBig5 — positive (modest confidence)
  - Twin measured many personality constructs overlapping the Big Five. Even though OpenPsych is a self-selected internet sample (different frame), direct trait overlap makes the persona prior likely beneficial for predicting trait-item response distributions at the population-slice level.

- OSPsychRWAS — positive (lower–moderate confidence)
  - RWAS is ideological/value-laden. Twin lacks an explicit, full political-ideology battery in the description but includes open-ended content and trait measures that correlate with authoritarianism. Some improvement expected but sample-frame differences and strong ideological content limit effect size.

- DICES — positive (lower confidence)
  - Safety judgments are influenced by demographics, social attitudes, and sensitivity to identity-related harms. Twin's demographic and personality signals can help, but safety judgments depend heavily on annotator context, platform norms, and item details—so only modest gains likely.

- Jester — positive (low confidence)
  - Humor/funniness is a taste/preference variable that correlates with personality/demographics (openness, age). Twin's personality measures can help predict taste distributions, but humor is idiosyncratic and Jester's original sample/frame differ from Twin, so effect is small/uncertain.

- ChaosNLI — insignificant (low–moderate confidence)
  - NLI judgments for ambiguous items are domain- and item-specific and heavily shaped by linguistic interpretation and local context. Twin's cognitive and education proxies might provide a small signal, but 100 annotators per item and the intrinsic ambiguity mean persona-level priors are unlikely to produce a clearly meaningful improvement over a strong direct baseline.

- OSPsychMGKT — insignificant (low confidence)
  - General-knowledge correctness depends on domain-specific exposure and factual knowledge. Twin provides cognitive summaries and some demographics (education proxies), but lacks the detailed topical knowledge that predicts yes/no subitems across many domains. Effects are likely small and noisy.

- OSPsychMACH — insignificant (low confidence)
  - Machiavellianism is a personality construct Twin might partly cover, but only one U.S.-eligible overlap row and a large OpenPsych sample-frame mismatch make any transfer effect unclear and likely not clearly meaningful.

- ISSP — insignificant (low confidence)
  - ISSP items are tightly bound to country-specific institutional framing and survey-wave context. Even though Twin is U.S.-based, the importance of survey-year/institutional language and cross-national comparability reduces the usefulness of Twin persona priors; any gains may be outweighed by contextual mismatch, so no clear effect is expected.
