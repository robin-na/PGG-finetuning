# Social Media Moral Judgment Dataset

## Citation
Vahed, S., Goanta, C., Ortolani, P., & Sanfey, A. G. (2024). Moral judgment of objectionable online content: Reporting decisions and punishment preferences on social media. *PLOS ONE*, 19(3), e0300960.

## Source
- **OSF**: https://osf.io/4ygj3/
- **DOI**: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0300960

## Recruitment
- **Platform**: Prolific
- **Country/region**: EU member states (multi-country); English fluency required
- **Age**: 18+; active social media user (monthly+)
- **Sample**: n=300 recruited, n=294 analyzed after exclusions
- **Groups**: Control (n=149) vs Responsibility (n=145)
- **Collection dates**: June 7-8, 2022

## Payment Structure (Non-IC)
Participants compensated via Prolific at mean 10.04 GBP/hr (~25 min). Task outcomes (reporting choice, punishment slider) do not determine participant payoffs. Decisions are purely hypothetical from a payment perspective.

## Design
- **Task type**: Single-participant repeated content moderation decisions
- **Judgment trials**: 66 trials with 3-choice action (Like / Dislike / Report)
- **Punishment trials**: 36 trials with slider mapping to ban length (0 to 30+ days)
- **Between-subjects**: "Content moderator responsibility" vs control
- **Within-subjects**: Image moral category, poster intention
- **Multiplayer**: No

## Key Variables
- `Report vs not` (often analyzed as Report vs Like/Dislike)
- Punishment severity (ban days via slider)
- Trait empathy measures
- Platform trust measures
- Condition assignment (responsibility vs control)

## Demographics
Age, gender recorded. Additional SES measures (education, income, ethnicity) not confirmed in methods.

## Ethics
Radboud University faculty ethics committee (ECSW-2022-011). Written consent obtained; participants warned about sensitive content.
